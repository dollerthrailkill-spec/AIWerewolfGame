*阅读以下段落，回答26-28题。*

段落A：
Historians once described the Enlightenment as a coherent project driven by a unified set of principles. Recent scholarship, however, paints a far more fractured picture. Rather than a single river, the Enlightenment now resembles a delta, with diverse currents of thought branching off, converging, and diverging again. This reinterpretation has profound implications: it suggests that the movement’s "contradictions" were not aberrations but intrinsic features, reflecting the messy, pluralistic reality of intellectual change.

**26.** The "delta" metaphor is used to emphasize the Enlightenment's:
A. geographical spread across Europe.
B. eventual decline and loss of influence.
C. fragmentation into multiple, sometimes conflicting, streams of thought.
D. overwhelming influence on subsequent intellectual history.
答案：C

**27.** The word "aberrations" most nearly means in the context:
A. fundamental characteristics
B. exceptions or departures from the norm
C. creative innovations
D. logical conclusions
答案：B

**28.** Which statement best aligns with the "recent scholarship" mentioned?
A. The Enlightenment possessed a singular, cohesive ideology.
B. Internal inconsistencies weaken the overall value of Enlightenment thought.
C. The divergences within the Enlightenment are central to understanding its nature.
D. Earlier historians completely misunderstood the chronological scope of the Enlightenment.
答案：C



*阅读以下段落，回答29-31题。*

段落B：
The concept of "emotional labor"—managing one's feelings to fulfill the emotional requirements of a job—was originally applied to service workers like flight attendants. However, with the rise of social media, emotional labor has crept into everyday life in unprecedented ways. We now curate our online personas, responding to strangers' grief and outrage with calculated empathy. This blurs the boundary between authentic feeling and performed emotion, leading to what some sociologists call a "commodification of affect." The danger, they argue, is not simply exhaustion but a pervasive sense of inauthenticity that can erode our most intimate relationships.

**29.** According to the passage, emotional labor in the age of social media differs chiefly in that it:
A. requires more physical effort than traditional service jobs.
B. is confined to professional environments and not private life.
C. extends beyond paid employment into daily personal interactions.
D. has been eliminated by the use of algorithmic curation.
答案：C

**30.** The phrase "commodification of affect" refers to:
A. the emotional exhaustion suffered by service workers.
B. the process of turning personal emotions into something exchanged or performed like a product.
C. a clinical condition diagnosed by sociologists.
D. the natural evolution of human empathy in the digital age.
答案：B

**31.** The ultimate concern raised by sociologists regarding this phenomenon is:
A. an increase in online fraud and impersonation.
B. the economic exploitation of social media users.
C. a loss of genuine feeling in personal bonds.

*阅读以下段落，回答32-34题。*

段落C：
In her study of coral reefs, marine biologist Dr. Ayana Okada moves beyond the usual focus on biodiversity loss. She argues that the prevailing narrative of reefs as fragile ecosystems in need of "saving" has inadvertently disempowered local communities that have coexisted with reefs for centuries. By portraying reefs as passive victims, conservation strategies often sideline the traditional ecological knowledge that has sustained these human-reef relationships. Okada advocates for a paradigm shift: instead of merely protecting reefs from people, we should learn from cultures that have lived with and shaped these environments through adaptive practices.

**32.** Dr. Okada's critique primarily targets:
A. the lack of marine protected areas worldwide.
B. the common framing of reefs as helpless entities that must be saved by outsiders.
C. scientific research that overstates the resilience of coral reefs.
D. local communities that exploit reef resources unsustainably.

A. given local people more authority over reef management.
B. ignored the active role and knowledge of people who have historically stewarded reefs.
C. successfully trained local populations in modern conservation science.
D. provided economic alternatives to fishing that are widely accepted.

*阅读以下段落，回答35-40题。*

段落D (长难文本摘要):
The paradox of modern autonomy lies in the simultaneous expansion of choice and the contraction of genuine agency. On the surface, digital platforms offer an almost limitless array of options—what to buy, whom to connect with, which version of reality to inhabit. Yet these choices are increasingly prefabricated by algorithms that predict, nudge, and ultimately shape our preferences. The individual, convinced of their sovereign will, is often acting out a script written by data patterns. This condition, which theorists call "algorithmic heteronomy," challenges the very foundation of liberal individualism. If our desires are not truly our own, can we be said to be free? The answer requires rethinking freedom not as the absence of external constraint, but as the capacity for critical self-reflection and the deliberate cultivation of tastes that resist automated optimization.
